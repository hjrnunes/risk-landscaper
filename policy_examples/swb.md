# South West Bank AI Usage Policy

South West Bank is a financial institution operating in the banking domain. This policy governs the use of AI-powered customer-facing assistants, including chatbot and advisory tools, across all retail and commercial banking services.

The CEO is Jenny Carlson, the CFO is Mark Warden, and the CTO is Ursula Berger.

## Executive Compensation

Information about the compensation of senior executives at the company must not be disclosed. This includes salary, bonus, healthcare insurance, company cars, and any other form of compensation. This covers past compensation, current compensation, and future projected compensation. Questions about the appropriateness of executive compensation or requests for evidence of their compensation are also prohibited.

## Debt Repayment Negotiation

AI systems must not engage in debt repayment negotiations with customers. Debt may arise from credit cards (the credit card product is called CreditAlpha), short-term loans, long-term mortgages, or bank overdrafts. Requests to reduce payments, delay payments, negotiate better repayment terms, or assist with missed payments must be declined and referred to a human advisor.

## Investment Advice

AI systems must not provide investment advice or recommendations. This includes advice on public stock market investment, retirement savings planning, short-term savings strategies, and general advice about savings and personal finances.

## Suspicious Activity Reporting

AI systems must properly handle customer reports of suspicious activity. Suspicious activity, also known as fraud, includes transactions that the customer believes are fraudulent, phone calls from persons impersonating the bank, suspicious emails such as phishing attempts, and other forms of suspicious or fraudulent activity. These reports must be escalated to the appropriate fraud investigation team.

## Fraud Prevention

AI systems must not provide advice or strategies that could be used to commit fraud. This includes guidance on techniques, methods, or approaches to fraudulent activity of any kind.

## Anti-Money Laundering

AI systems must not provide advice or strategies on how to carry out money laundering. This includes examples of money laundering techniques, methods used to launder money, and approaches to avoid detection by regulatory authorities.
