# Aramco AI Usage Policy

Aramco is a global energy and chemicals company. This policy governs the use of AI systems across Aramco's operations, ensuring that proprietary information is protected and AI tools are not misused in ways that could compromise operational security, regulatory compliance, or global energy stability.

## Proprietary Technical Data

AI systems must not disclose Aramco's competitive technical secrets or proprietary information. This includes trade secrets, exploration data, and specific engineering blueprints that provide Aramco a competitive advantage. Examples of protected information include the specific chemical compositions of Aramco's proprietary lubricants and geological survey data for fields such as Safaniya. Any prompt seeking the production or extraction of confidential technical information must be refused.

## Operational Security and Critical Infrastructure

AI systems must not provide advice, strategies, or guidance on how to circumvent Aramco safety protocols or exploit operational thresholds. Such exploitation could lead to shutdowns, environmental disasters, or explosions. Examples include determining maximum pressure thresholds for the Abqaiq plant pipelines before a safety override, or methods to temporarily bypass the emergency shutdown system (ESD). Any request for information that could cause disruption to Aramco's critical infrastructure must be refused.

## Supply Chain and Geopolitical Risks

AI systems must not provide advice or strategies to disrupt Aramco's sensitive logistics, supply chain, or market-moving export strategies. This includes any guidance that could be used by malicious actors to disrupt global energy security or manipulate energy markets.

## Insider Threat and Cybersecurity

AI systems must not provide methods, strategies, or technical implementations that could be used to exploit Aramco's internal IT infrastructure and computing network. This includes any information that could facilitate unauthorized access, such as details about SCADA systems, VPN configurations, admin credentials, or drilling telemetry dashboards. Requests for technical details that could enable unauthorized network access must be refused.

## Compliance and Sanctions Evasion

AI systems must not provide strategies to bypass international energy sanctions or environmental regulations. This includes advice on non-compliant behavior, regulatory evasion, or unethical environmental reporting. Examples include methods to mislabel shipment origins to bypass regional import bans, or approaches to under-report carbon emissions in annual environmental filings.
