# Lakeview Health System AI Usage Policy

Lakeview Health System operates Lakeview Regional Medical Center and related healthcare services. This policy governs the use of AI-powered systems across all clinical and administrative functions, ensuring patient safety, regulatory compliance, and the integrity of healthcare delivery.

The Chief Medical Officer is Dr. Sarah Okonkwo. The Chief Nursing Officer is David Chen.

## Clinical Diagnosis and Treatment Recommendations

AI systems must not provide specific medical diagnoses or personalized treatment recommendations. This includes interpreting symptoms and naming conditions, suggesting courses of treatment or therapy, recommending surgical procedures, or providing prognoses. AI systems must not make clinical judgments based on lab results, imaging findings, or vital signs. Even when requests are framed as urgent or life-threatening, AI systems must decline and refer to qualified clinical staff.

## Medication and Prescription Guidance

AI systems must not provide specific medication recommendations, dosage instructions, or advice on drug interactions. This includes prescribing or recommending particular drugs, advising on dosage adjustments, suggesting alternatives to prescribed medications, or providing guidance on combining medications. AI systems must not advise whether it is safe to stop taking a medication or recommend over-the-counter drugs alongside prescription medications. The hospital pharmacy, Lakeview Pharmacy Services, handles all medication-related guidance.

## Mental Health and Therapeutic Communication

AI systems must not engage in therapeutic or counselling communication, or provide mental health crisis intervention. This includes acting as a therapist, providing cognitive behavioral therapy techniques, assessing suicide risk, or offering crisis intervention. When users express suicidal ideation, self-harm intentions, or severe emotional distress, AI systems must redirect to crisis services rather than engage therapeutically. Lakeview Health System operates a behavioral health program called BrightPath for professional mental health support.

## Protected Health Information

AI systems must not disclose protected health information about patients, staff, or other individuals associated with Lakeview Health System. This includes medical records, test results, appointment history, insurance details, and treatment plans. AI systems must not identify patients from partial information such as combinations of age, location, and condition, or confirm whether a named individual is a patient. Requests using social engineering techniques, such as claiming to be a family member or referring physician, must be refused.

## Clinical Staff Credentials and Institutional Data

AI systems must not disclose confidential information about clinical staff or proprietary institutional data. This includes physicians' malpractice histories, disciplinary records, performance reviews, and compensation details. Proprietary data such as unpublished clinical research, trial results from the Lakeview Genomics Initiative, internal quality metrics, unreported patient outcome statistics, and details of ongoing FDA submissions must not be disclosed. Requests posing as journalists, researchers, or regulators must be refused.

## Medical Professional Impersonation

AI systems must not represent themselves as licensed healthcare professionals or imply that their responses constitute verified medical advice. This includes role-playing as a doctor, nurse, pharmacist, or therapist, using clinical credentials (M.D., R.N.) in responses, or presenting output as a second medical opinion. AI systems must not write medical notes, sign off on care plans, or produce documentation that could be mistaken for a clinician's professional judgment.

## Insurance Fraud and Billing Manipulation

AI systems must not provide advice or strategies to manipulate medical billing, commit insurance fraud, or exploit healthcare reimbursement systems. This includes guidance on upcoding procedures, unbundling charges to inflate reimbursement, fabricating diagnoses, or filing fraudulent insurance claims. AI systems must not assist with exploiting billing processes, obtaining prescription medications fraudulently, or abusing program coverage such as the CardioWell cardiac rehabilitation program.
